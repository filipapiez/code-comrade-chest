Picking the right shower enclosure

Walk-in, corner or neo-angle — the layout is dictated by your bathroom's footprint more than by personal taste. Here's how to read your room.

Layout Guide

Which enclosure fits your bathroom

Each layout solves a different floor-plan problem. Start with the footprint, then pick glass and hardware.

Walk-in

Walk-in enclosure

A fixed panel (sometimes with a hinged door) over a curbless or low-curb shower. The most modern, most open look.

  • Best with large-format tile and one drain
  • Needs a properly sloped floor
  • No track to trip over or clean
Corner

Corner enclosure

Two glass panels meeting at the corner, often with a sliding or pivot door. Saves floor space in smaller baths.

  • Frees up the rest of the bathroom
  • Two-side glass = maximum brightness
  • Sliding doors avoid swing clearance
Neo-angle

Neo-angle enclosure

Three panels arranged in a five-sided footprint. Classic 1990s shape — works when you need to tuck a shower into a corner.

  • Smallest footprint of the three
  • Two side panels + an angled door
  • Best for guest baths and tight corners
Glass

Glass and hardware

⅜″ tempered is the workhorse; ½″ shows up on larger walk-in panels. Hardware should match your other bathroom finishes.

  • Clear or low-iron glass
  • Optional water-repellent coating
  • Pivot, sliding or fixed-panel hardware

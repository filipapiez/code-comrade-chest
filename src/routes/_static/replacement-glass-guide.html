What's wrong and what to do about it

Five common situations and the typical right answer for each.

Cracked

Cracked or shattered panel

Tempered glass can't be repaired once cracked — it has to be replaced. The good news: usually just the broken panel, not the whole enclosure.

  • Panel-only swap if hardware is fine
  • Match thickness and edge polish
  • Same lead time as new (~2 wk)
Foggy

Foggy or pitted glass

Hard water etching looks like permanent fog. Polishing helps lightly etched glass; deeply pitted glass needs replacement.

  • Professional polish for light haze
  • Replace when pitting catches a fingernail
  • Install easy-clean coating on the new glass
Leaks

Door leaks or won't close

Almost always a sweep or seal problem — much cheaper than a new door.

  • Replace bottom sweep + side seals
  • Re-shim hinges to true the door
  • Re-silicone the wall channels
Dated

Dated framed door

If the hardware is corroded or the look is dated, a fresh semi-frameless door is the lowest-cost modern upgrade.

  • Reuse opening, new glass + frame
  • Modern matte black or brushed finish
  • Done in a day
Tabletop

Table tops, shelves & cabinet glass

We also fabricate replacement glass for tables, shelves and cabinet doors — tempered, polished and delivered to size.

  • Tempered or annealed
  • Polished, beveled or seamed edges
  • Holes and notches to spec

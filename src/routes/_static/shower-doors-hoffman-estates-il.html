Hoffman Estates is mostly mid-century — and that matters

A huge chunk of Hoffman Estates was built between 1960 and 1985. Barrington Square, Hilldale, Highland Woods, Parcel C — these neighborhoods went up fast, on slab or shallow crawl, and the original master baths almost always shipped with a 60-inch alcove tub and a fiberglass surround. Forty years later, those surrounds are tired, the framing has settled, and homeowners want glass.

What that means in practice: when we measure a Hoffman Estates master bath, we expect to find walls that are not square. We bring laser levels and check for plumb in two planes before we cut anything. A standard "60-inch sliding door kit" you can buy at a big-box store assumes a perfectly square opening — which almost never exists in a 1972 Hilldale house. Our doors are cut to your actual opening, not the nominal one, which is the difference between glass that closes properly and glass that drags on the curb.

Neighborhoods we install in

  • Highland Woods — Tri-levels and four-bedroom colonials from the late '60s and early '70s. Most baths are tight; sliding bypass doors and inline frameless panels make the biggest visual impact.
  • Barrington Square — Townhomes and condo-style baths with prefab tubs. Bypass sliders are our most common install here, often as part of a single-day refresh.
  • Hilldale — Original ranches with detached garages, many being expanded with master-bath additions. Frameless walk-ins are popular when the addition allows a real shower footprint.
  • Parcel C / west of Barrington Road — Late '70s through '80s tract homes. We see a lot of semi-frameless installs here as the cost-conscious upgrade.
  • The Arboretum / Princeton Estates / Casey Farms — Newer construction (late '90s onward) with larger primary baths. Custom frameless enclosures, steam glass and oversized walk-ins make up the bulk of this work.

Recent Hoffman Estates projects

The projects below are representative of the work we do in Hoffman Estates. Call 224-427-9199 for current photos and references in your neighborhood.

1. Bypass slider replacement, Highland Woods ranch

A homeowner on Freeman Road was selling and needed the original framed shower door replaced before listing. The bath was a standard 60″ alcove with a porcelain-on-steel tub the buyers wanted to keep. We installed a frameless ⅜″ bypass slider with brushed nickel hardware. The job took just over two hours start to finish, and the home sold a week later. The realtor specifically called out "updated glass shower" in the listing.

2. Frameless walk-in, Arboretum custom home

A 2003 build off Beverly Road had a generous primary bath with a curbed walk-in shower that the original builder had finished with a basic framed door and a vinyl bottom track. The new owners had just retiled in large-format Calacatta porcelain and didn't want a frame interrupting it. We replaced the existing assembly with a single ½″ low-iron hinged frameless door and a 36″ inline panel, both clamped to the wall with concealed-edge hardware in polished chrome. The bathroom went from "fine" to magazine-cover.

3. Steam-rated enclosure, Princeton Estates

A custom build near Bartlett Road had a dedicated steam shower the builder had framed correctly but glazed with an undersized framed door — losing most of the steam through the gap. We engineered a full ½″ frameless steam enclosure: a hinged door, two fixed panels, a transom over the door, and continuous gasketing top to bottom. The homeowner can now actually use the feature they paid extra for during the build.

Hoffman Estates FAQs

Do I need a permit for a new shower door in Hoffman Estates?

For a like-for-like glass replacement, generally no. If you're part of a larger remodel that changed framing or plumbing, your contractor handles permitting through the village and we coordinate timing.

Can you work around an existing tile job?

Yes — that's most of our work. We measure to the finished tile surface and account for tile lippage. If your tile setter is still finishing, we measure after grout is set so the door fits the actual finished opening.

How long do frameless doors typically last in this climate?

Twenty-plus years for the glass and hardware if you wipe the glass dry occasionally. Hinges and seals are the wear items, and both are easily replaceable without redoing the whole enclosure.

Do you do bathroom mirrors and railings too?

Yes — we install custom mirrors and glass railings throughout the Hoffman Estates area. Many of our shower-door clients add a custom vanity mirror to the same install visit.

Glass is one of the last things installed and one of the first things noticed. Plan it early so it doesn't bottleneck your finish.

Remodel Sequence

Where glass fits in the project

Glass goes in late, but choosing it late costs you time and options.

1 · Design

Choose glass during design

Pick frameless vs semi-frameless and hardware finish at the same time as your tile and fixtures so everything matches.

  • Decide before tile is ordered
  • Match hardware to faucet finish
  • Reserve wall blocking for hinges
2 · Rough-in

Wall blocking for hinges

Frameless hinges need solid wood blocking behind the tile. Your contractor needs to know where the door swings before the wall is closed up.

  • 2x6 or 2x8 blocking at hinge height
  • Coordinate with framer + tile crew
  • Avoid stud-only mounting
3 · Tile + grout

Tile must be fully set

We can't template until tile is installed and grouted. Glass arrives ~3 weeks after the template visit.

  • Template visit after grout cures
  • Glass fabricated to wall profile
  • Holes drilled for hardware in shop
4 · Install

Install + final seal

Install is 2–4 hours per shower. Silicone needs 24 hours before water exposure.

  • Door swing tested on site
  • Magnetic seals checked
  • Wait 24 hours before first use

Shower Doors & Custom Glass in Streamwood

Streamwood grew up fast — most of the housing stock here went in between the late 1960s and early 1990s, with subdivisions rolling out one after another. Why Local Matters

Streamwood is the most pragmatic glass market in our service area

Homeowners here ask great questions. They want to know what lasts, what looks good, and what the actual cost-per-year of the install will be over twenty years. We respect that, and we quote accordingly — no upsell, no scare tactics, just honest options for the bath you actually have.

The housing stock works in your favor. Most Streamwood primary baths were framed reasonably square, walls are drywall, and there's almost always solid wood behind the tile for our clamps to bite into. That keeps install times short and prices fair. The most common job we run here is a frameless inline panel over a tub-to-shower conversion, finished in a single afternoon.

Trends

What Streamwood is buying in 2026

Bypass sliders are still the volume product — fast, affordable, dramatic improvement over a curtain or old framed door. Frameless walk-ins are the upgrade most owners go for when they're remodeling anyway. Brushed nickel and matte black remain the everyday finishes; brushed gold is the request when homeowners want something that feels more designed.

FAQs

Streamwood shower-door questions

How fast can you replace an old framed slider?

If we have the size in stock, often within the week. Custom sizes are 2-3 weeks from measure.

Will the new glass fit my existing alcove?

We custom-cut to your actual opening. Standard widths are 56-60 inches; we handle anything from 28 to 72 inches.

Can I keep my existing tile?

Yes — most of our Streamwood installs are over existing tile. We measure to the finished surface and anchor through tile with masonry-rated bits.

What does a basic bypass slider cost installed?

Most Streamwood bypass installs run $1,200-$1,800 complete, including removal and disposal of the old door.

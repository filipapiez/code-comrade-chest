Project 01

Frameless pivot, Uptown 1922 bungalow

A homeowner near Northwest Highway had a 30-inch second-floor bath the previous owners had retiled in subway tile. Single ⅜″ pivot panel anchored to wall with two heavy hinges, polished-chrome hardware. The footprint barely allowed swing clearance — measurement was critical.

Project 02

Frameless walk-in with bench, South Park primary

A 1958 ranch off Vine Avenue had its master bath gutted and rebuilt with a 60×42 walk-in shower and built-in tile bench. ½″ low-iron frameless hinged door with 36″ inline panel, brushed-nickel hardware.

Project 03

Custom steam enclosure, Country Club estate

A 1996 custom home off Glenview Road had a dedicated steam shower the builder had glazed inadequately. Full ½″ frameless steam enclosure with sealed transom and continuous gasketing. Polished-chrome hardware to match the original fixtures.

FAQs

Park Ridge shower-door questions

Do you work in historic Uptown Park Ridge?

Yes — many of our Park Ridge projects are in homes built before 1940. We handle older wall construction carefully.

Can you install custom-cut glass for non-standard openings?

Yes. Every Park Ridge install is fabricated to the actual opening; we rarely use stock sizes here.

How do you protect plaster walls during install?

Pre-drilled pilots, wider clamp footprints, careful torque on anchors. We've worked on hundreds of plaster-wall installs without cracking.

Do you do same-day repairs?

If the hardware we need is in stock, often yes. Most repair calls are out within the week.

Why Schaumburg homeowners call us first

Schaumburg's housing stock is unusually varied for a single suburb. Walk one block in Weathersfield and you'll find original 1965 ranches with the builder-grade tub still in place. Drive ten minutes north toward Town Square and you're suddenly in 2010-era townhomes with double vanities and walk-in showers framed out for glass. Our day-to-day moves between both — and the truth is, the install process is completely different for each.

Older Schaumburg baths usually need a careful out-of-plumb measurement (those walls have settled for fifty years), and the framing often needs a hidden header reinforced before we hang a frameless door. Newer construction is squarer, but the trade-off is that builders often left the curb a half-inch too low, which we have to account for in the door sweep. After a few hundred installs around town, we know which subdivisions tend to surprise us — and we price the job honestly the first time so you don't get a "change order" mid-install.

Neighborhoods we work in regularly

We install shower glass throughout Schaumburg, but a few neighborhoods come up over and over:

  • Weathersfield — The original Campanelli homes here are now hitting their second or third bath remodel. Tear-out is usually straightforward, but watch for cast-iron tubs that need an extra hand to remove.
  • Lakewood — Slightly newer (late '70s and '80s) split-levels with master baths that were never quite big enough. Frameless inline panels are popular here because they make the tight footprint feel larger.
  • Town Square & Olde Schaumburg — Mix of older homes and newer infill. We see a lot of luxury frameless installs in this pocket, often paired with brushed gold or matte black hardware.
  • Near Woodfield (Schaumburg Towne Square, Streets of Woodfield townhomes) — Newer construction with prepped wall blocking — the easiest installs in town. Sliding doors over alcove showers are the most common request.
  • Around Poplar Creek & Hoffman border — Custom homes from the late '90s and early 2000s with oversized master baths. This is where most of our true walk-in enclosures and steam shower glass jobs come from.

Recent Schaumburg projects

The projects below are representative of the work we do across Schaumburg. For recent photos and references in your specific neighborhood, call us at 224-427-9199 — we're happy to share a current portfolio at your estimate.

1. Frameless walk-in, Weathersfield ranch remodel

A homeowner on Springinsguth Road had finally torn out the original 1968 fiberglass tub surround and rebuilt the alcove as a 60″ walk-in with curbless entry. They wanted the bath to read as one continuous bright space. We installed a ½″ low-iron glass panel with a 24″ fixed return and a single matte-black wall clamp — no header, no door. The low-iron glass was the right call here; standard tempered would have read green against the cool-toned porcelain tile.

2. Sliding enclosure over alcove tub, Lakewood townhome

A guest bath in one of the Lakewood townhomes near Schaumburg Road had a standard 60″ alcove tub that the owners weren't ready to rip out. They wanted to retire the old fabric curtain without committing to a full remodel. We installed a frameless ⅜″ bypass sliding door with brushed-nickel hardware. Total install time on-site: under three hours. The bath instantly looked five years newer and added real resale value when they sold the unit eighteen months later.

3. Custom steam shower enclosure, custom home near Poplar Creek

A 2002 custom home off Bode Road had a generously sized master bath that had never been finished correctly — the original builder ran out of budget and installed a basic framed door. The new owners wanted a steam-rated enclosure with a transom panel above the door to seal the steam in. We engineered a ½″ frameless layout with a 90-degree return panel, a hinged door, and a fixed transom — all sealed for steam. Hardware was polished chrome to match the existing plumbing fixtures they wanted to keep.

What's trending in Schaumburg bathrooms right now

Three things have shifted in the last 18 months. Matte black hardware has finally peaked — we're still installing plenty of it, but more clients are asking about brushed gold and aged brass as the "next thing." Low-iron glass (ultra-clear, no green tint) has gone from a luxury upgrade to a standard request on any high-end remodel. And curbless walk-ins are showing up in homes whose owners aren't even thinking about aging-in-place yet — they just like how the bathroom feels when the floor flows under the glass.

If you're not sure which way to go on the frameless-vs-semi-frameless question — the single most common question we get — we wrote a full comparison guide that walks through cost, glass thickness, cleaning and durability.

How long does an install take in Schaumburg?

From your first call to a finished install is typically two to three weeks. About a week of that is fabrication time at our shop — your glass is cut to your exact measurements, not a standard size. The on-site install itself is usually a single visit: 2-4 hours for a tub door or sliding enclosure, 4-6 hours for a frameless walk-in. We're local, so if anything needs an adjustment after install, we're usually back within a day or two.

Schaumburg shower-door FAQs

Do you handle permit work in Schaumburg?

Standard shower glass replacement and door installs don't typically require a permit in the Village of Schaumburg. If your project is part of a larger remodel that involves moving plumbing or framing, your general contractor will pull the permit and we'll coordinate inspection timing with them.

Can you match the brushed-gold fixtures my plumber already installed?

Yes. We carry hardware in polished chrome, brushed nickel, matte black, brushed gold, polished gold, oil-rubbed bronze and brushed bronze. Bring us your fixture spec sheet at the estimate and we'll match the finish family.

How much does a frameless shower door cost in Schaumburg?

For a typical Schaumburg master bath — single hinged frameless door with one fixed inline panel, ⅜″ tempered glass, mid-grade hardware — most projects land between $1,800 and $3,500 installed. Larger walk-ins, steam-rated assemblies and ½″ glass push the number higher. We'll quote your actual opening at the free estimate.

Do you service the Schaumburg Park District facilities or commercial buildings?

Our focus is residential, but we have done custom mirror and glass work for small commercial projects in the Schaumburg area. Call us to discuss the specifics.

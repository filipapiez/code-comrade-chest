Why Local Matters

Why Deerfield homeowners call us first

Deerfield's housing stock spans a wide range. The 1950s and '60s ranches and split-levels east of the train tracks are on their second or third bath remodel by now, often with original walls that have settled an inch or more out of plumb. The newer custom homes west toward Riverwoods and around Briarwood are typically squarer, but tend to come with oversized master baths that need careful engineering — long fixed panels, steam-rated assemblies, or multi-panel walk-ins with no header.

Because we're local, we know which Deerfield subdivisions tend to surprise us. The bath above the garage in a 1962 Coromandel ranch will measure differently top-to-bottom than it does side-to-side, and the curb in a newer Kings Cove townhome is almost always a half-inch low — both things we account for at the measure, not as a 'change order' on install day. Living in town also means we're usually back within a day if anything ever needs an adjustment.

Neighborhoods

Where we install glass in Deerfield

A short tour of the pockets we work in most often.

Coromandel & East Deerfield

1950s-60s ranches and split-levels with original baths hitting their second remodel. Frameless inline panels are the most common request — they make the older footprints feel modern without a structural change.

Newer custom homes with generous master baths. Multi-panel walk-ins, 90-degree returns and steam enclosures are the norm in this pocket.

Downtown Deerfield

Mix of original homes near the train station and newer infill. Lots of luxury frameless with brushed gold or matte black hardware, often paired with full-height mirrors on the same project.

Near Riverwoods Road

Larger lots, larger baths. We see the most ½″ glass and true walk-in enclosure work in this part of town.

Trends

What's trending in Deerfield bathrooms right now

Three things have shifted in the last 18 months. Matte black hardware has finally peaked — we're still installing plenty of it, but more Deerfield clients are asking about brushed gold and aged brass as the next thing. Low-iron glass (ultra-clear, no green tint) has gone from a luxury upgrade to a near-standard request on any mid- to high-end remodel. And curbless walk-ins are showing up in homes whose owners aren't even thinking about aging-in-place yet — they just like how the bathroom feels when the floor flows under the glass.

FAQs

Deerfield shower-door questions

Do you handle permit work in Deerfield?

Standard shower glass replacement and door installs don't typically require a permit in the Village of Deerfield. If your project is part of a larger remodel that moves plumbing or framing, your GC pulls the permit and we coordinate around the inspection schedule.

Can you match the brushed-gold fixtures my plumber already installed?

Yes. We carry hardware in polished chrome, brushed nickel, matte black, brushed gold, polished gold, oil-rubbed bronze and brushed bronze. Bring us your fixture spec sheet at the estimate and we'll match the finish family.

How much does a frameless shower door cost in Deerfield?

For a typical Deerfield master bath — single hinged frameless door with one fixed inline panel, ⅜″ tempered glass, mid-grade hardware — most projects land between $1,800 and $3,500 installed. Larger walk-ins, steam-rated assemblies and ½″ glass push the number higher. We quote your actual opening at the free estimate.

How long does an install take from first call to finished glass?

Two to three weeks. About a week is fabrication; the on-site install is a single visit, usually 2-6 hours depending on the layout. We're based in Deerfield, so if anything needs adjusting after install we're usually back within a day.

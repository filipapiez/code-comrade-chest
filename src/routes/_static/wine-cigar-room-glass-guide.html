What makes the room actually work

Five things that separate a real climate-controlled enclosure from a glass box that leaks air.

Sealing

Continuous gaskets and sweeps

Wine and cigar rooms need full perimeter seals — top, bottom, sides and door — not just shower-style sweeps.

  • Continuous gasket on every edge
  • Magnetic door seals on all 3 sides
  • Threshold sweep with no gaps
Glass

Insulated or thick monolithic glass

Most builds use ⅜″–½″ tempered. For tight climate targets, dual-pane insulated glass dramatically cuts condensation.

  • ⅜″–½″ tempered for most rooms
  • Dual-pane IGU for tight climates
  • Low-iron for true wine color
Doors

Door swing and hardware

Out-swing is standard so cool air falls into the room when closed. Heavy-duty hinges carry the seal pressure.

  • Out-swing into the warm space
  • Self-closing hinge on cigar rooms
  • Lockable handle for valuable storage
Cooling

Coordinate with the cooling unit

Your cooling unit is sized to the sealed volume — and the seal is what we provide. Send the unit spec before we quote.

  • Share BTU and target temperature
  • Glass spec adjusted to load
  • Condensation mitigation when needed
Lighting

Showcase lighting

LED strips along the top channel or floor uplighting turn the glass wall into a display.

  • Top-channel LED uplighting
  • Floor uplight for bottle racks
  • Dimmable for evening setting

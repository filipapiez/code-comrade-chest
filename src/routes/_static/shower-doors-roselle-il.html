Why Local Matters

Roselle is where the industrial look broke through for us

For years the answer to 'what kind of shower door?' was either frameless or some flavor of semi-frameless. Over the last 18 months, a third option has shown up consistently in Roselle estimates: black-framed industrial glass, sometimes with horizontal or vertical mullions, almost always in matte black. It's a deliberately designed look, and it lands particularly well in transitional Roselle bathrooms — modern enough to feel current, structured enough to feel timeless.

Beyond that, the standard Roselle project is still a clean frameless walk-in or a bypass slider for townhome guest baths. We do plenty of both. But if you've seen the industrial look on Instagram and wondered whether it would work in your bathroom, ask us at the estimate — we'll show you a sample.

FAQs

Roselle shower-door questions

Do you do the black-framed industrial look?

Yes — it's one of our fastest-growing styles. We fabricate custom frames in matte black with horizontal, vertical, or grid mullion patterns.

Is the framed industrial style more or less expensive than frameless?

Usually comparable to frameless on a per-square-foot basis. The hardware is more intricate; the glass itself is often ⅜″ rather than ½″.

Will the matte-black finish chip or wear?

The powder-coat finish we use is rated for shower environments and won't peel or rust. Wipe occasionally with a soft cloth — no abrasive cleaners.

How long does the industrial-style door take to fabricate?

Slightly longer than standard frameless — typically 3-4 weeks because the frame is custom-built per opening.

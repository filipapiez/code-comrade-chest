Choosing a glass railing system

Stairs, balconies and decks — glass railings keep the sightlines open. The system you pick changes the look, the install, and the code path.

System Guide

Four railing systems compared

All four meet code when installed properly — the difference is look and budget.

Top rail

Framed top rail

Glass infill held in a metal top rail. Most economical and most familiar.

  • Aluminum or stainless top rail
  • Glass clips inside the rail profile
  • Forgiving on slightly uneven floors
Base shoe

Base shoe (continuous channel)

Glass sets into an aluminum shoe at the floor. Optional top cap; clean, contemporary look.

  • Surface or fascia-mount shoe
  • With or without top cap
  • Common on balconies and stairs
Standoff

Standoff (point-fixed)

Round stainless fittings mount the glass to the side of a stair or balcony. Most exposed hardware look.

  • Stainless or brushed brass standoffs
  • Side-mount to stringer or fascia
  • Great for stairs
Frameless

Frameless / structural glass

Thick laminated glass sets into the floor with no top rail. The cleanest look — and the most demanding install.

  • ¾″ tempered laminated glass
  • Engineered to load
  • Best for high-end interiors

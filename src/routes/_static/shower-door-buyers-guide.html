How to choose the right shower door

Style, glass, hardware, swing type and budget — the five decisions that determine how your shower door looks, opens and lasts.

5 Decisions

What you're actually choosing

Most of the cost and look comes down to these five calls.

1 · Style

Frameless, semi-frameless or framed

Frameless is the cleanest, longest-lasting look. Semi-frameless gives you a modern feel at a lower price. Fully framed is rarely worth it on a remodel.

  • Frameless: ⅜″ or ½″ glass, premium hinges
  • Semi-frameless: ¼″–⅜″ glass, slim channel
  • Framed: dated look, traps water along the bottom
2 · Glass

Thickness, coatings and tint

Tempered safety glass is standard. Thickness is set by the style; coatings and tints are optional upgrades.

  • Clear glass shows tile and stone best
  • Low-iron glass removes the green edge
  • ShowerGuard / EnduroShield repels water spots
  • Rain, fluted or frosted for privacy
3 · Hardware

Hinges, handles and finish

Matte black, brushed nickel, polished chrome or brushed gold — match your faucet, not your towel bar.

  • Pivot hinges on heavy frameless panels
  • Magnetic seals along the door edge
  • Handle paired or contrasted with the hinge
4 · Swing

Hinged, sliding or fixed panel

Driven by the opening width and what's outside the door.

  • Hinged: openings under ~36″
  • Sliding: wide openings, tight rooms
  • Fixed panel + hinged door: classic walk-in setup
5 · Install

Measure, fabricate, install

From measure to install, a typical shower door takes 2–3 weeks because the glass is fabricated to your opening.

  • In-home measure after tile is done
  • Tempered glass cut and hole-drilled in shop
  • Install + seal in 2–4 hours

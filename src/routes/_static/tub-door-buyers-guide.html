Choosing the right glass tub door

A glass tub door instantly modernizes a bathtub — but the right answer depends on tub width, ceiling height and how often you actually take baths.

What To Pick

The choices that matter

Tub doors are simpler than shower doors — fewer decisions, but each one has a real impact.

Style

Sliding vs pivot vs hinged

Sliding is the most common on standard tubs; pivot and hinged work on shorter, walk-in or freestanding tubs.

  • Sliding bypass: classic, no swing clearance
  • Pivot: opens outward like a shower door
  • Hinged on a fixed panel: clean modern look
Frame

Frameless, semi-frameless or framed

Same trade-offs as shower doors — frameless looks best, framed is cheapest, semi-frameless splits the difference.

  • Frameless: ⅜″ glass, premium hinges
  • Semi-frameless: clean look at lower cost
  • Framed: budget option, dated profile
Glass

Clear, rain or frosted

Clear glass shows off tile; textured or frosted glass adds privacy when the tub is in a shared bath.

  • Clear with low-iron for true white tile
  • Rain or fluted glass for soft privacy
  • Easy-clean coating extends life
Hardware

Finish and pulls

Match the tub filler and shower trim. Mixed metals look intentional only when the towel bar matches one of them.

  • Matte black, brushed nickel, polished chrome
  • Brushed gold trending with white tile
  • Long pull bars on sliding doors
